Champion's Report

In Boston, Germany looks to secure a ticket to the round of 16 of the 2026 World Cup: Germans fired up against the Paraguayan wall

The 2026 World Cup enters its scorching knockout stage with a thrilling do-or-die clash. This evening, Monday, June 29, 2026, at 22:30 CEST (16:30 local time), the spotlight will turn to the prestigious Boston Stadium (the famous Gillette Stadium in Foxborough, Massachusetts). At stake is a crucial ticket to the round of 16, in a match that will see the powerhouse Germany face the proverbial defensive solidity of Paraguay. A fascinating tactical battle between the absolute quality of the German national team and the competitive grit of the South American wall.

Team form: stellar attack against bunker defense

Germany, led by Julian Nagelsmann, advanced from the group stage by convincingly winning Group E. The Germans displayed outstanding form, driven by a stellar attack that produced gems like the resounding 7-1 thrashing of Curaçao. However, the Teutonic selection comes into this round of 32 with the obligation to redeem their slip-up in the final round, a 2-1 defeat against Ecuador that raised some doubts about their defensive stability. On the other side of the pitch, Paraguay stands as one of the toughest opponents to break down. Qualifying as one of the best third-placed teams from Group D, Gustavo Alfaro's squad built their entire fortune on an incredibly tight defense and ironclad tactical organization. In their last two group matches, the Albirroja literally locked down their goal against tricky sides like Turkey and Australia, compensating for a low-scoring frontline that has found the net only twice since the start of the tournament.

Team news: heavy injuries and luxury returns

The eve of the match was shaken by major last-minute updates from both locker rooms. On the German side, there is a massive blow: center-back Nico Schlotterbeck's World Cup has unfortunately come to an early end due to a severe ligament injury to his left ankle sustained in the last match. This absence will force Nagelsmann to reshape his central defense. As for Paraguay, midfielder Diego Gómez will be forced to miss the clash through suspension due to the accumulation of yellow cards in the group stage. A heavy loss, but it is balanced by highly positive news: the official return of star player Miguel Almirón. The playmaker has served his ban and is ready to retake his place on the wing to spearhead the South American counterattacks.

Probable Lineups in Boston

Both coaches are finalizing their tactical decisions. Julian Nagelsmann will rely on the pure quality of his attacking midfielders to break down the tight opponent lines, while Alfaro will prepare a true wall of muscle in midfield.

Germany (4-2-3-1): Neuer; Kimmich, Tah, Rüdiger, Raum (or Brown); Nmecha, Pavlovic; Sané, Musiala, Wirtz; Havertz.

Coach: Julian Nagelsmann


Paraguay (4-4-2 / 4-2-3-1): Gill; Cáceres, Velázquez, Gómez, Alonso; Almirón, Cubas, Galarza, Maidana (or Bobadilla); Enciso, Pitta (or Ávalos).

Coach: Gustavo Alfaro

Historical Precedent: The Rematch of Korea/Japan 2002

World Cup statistics remind us that these two national teams have crossed paths on the world stage only once in history, exactly 24 years ago. We have to go back to the round of 16 of Korea/Japan 2002: on that occasion too, Germany were heavy favorites but faced a completely locked and grueling match against the Paraguayan backline. The Albirroja's wall only crumbled in the 88th minute thanks to a decisive strike from Oliver Neuville for the final 1-0, which propelled the Germans toward the final later lost to Brazil. Tonight in Boston, Paraguay will look to rewrite history, while Germany aims to maintain its favorable tradition in do-or-die matches.
