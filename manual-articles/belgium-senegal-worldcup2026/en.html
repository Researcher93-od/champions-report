Belgium hosts the Senegalese led by Thiaw in Seattle: a maturity test for the Red Devils for a spot in the Round of 16

By: Champion's Report Journal Published: July 1, 2026 Updated: 16:00 CEST

Seattle: the Red Devils chase the Round of 16 ticket against the dangerous African champions.

Here are the complete details for the upcoming match at 22:00 CEST between Belgium and Senegal, valid for the Round of 32 of the 2026 World Cup at the iconic Seattle Stadium. Both national teams head into this crucial knockout stage following resounding and high-scoring victories in their final group stage fixtures, sparking great excitement among their fans.

Match Context and Background

Historical Precedents: This is a historic and completely unprecedented encounter; the two sides have never faced each other before in an official competition, making this fixture highly unpredictable.

Belgium Form Guide: Rudi Garcia's men won Group G after pulling off an impressive comeback. Following two low-key draws against Egypt (1-1) and Iran (0-0), they unleashed their full power in the final game by defeating New Zealand 5-1, thanks to brilliant performances from Leandro Trossard and Kevin De Bruyne.

Senegal Form Guide: The Lions of Teranga qualified as one of the best third-placed teams. Defeated in their opening rounds by France (3-1) and Norway (3-2), they secured their knockout spot by crushing Iraq 5-0, driven by a stellar brace from Pape Gueye.

Tactical Keys to the Match

Belgium is expected to maintain their dynamic attacking shape with Charles De Ketelaere operating as a false nine, leaving experienced striker Romelu Lukaku ready to make an impact off the bench if needed. Senegal will respond with a compact defensive structure, looking to exploit lightning-fast vertical transitions led by Sadio Mané and Ismaïla Sarr (who has scored three goals so far). Furthermore, the African side will have to cope without their starting goalkeeper Édouard Mendy, with Mory Diaw stepping up between the posts.
